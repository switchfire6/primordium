PRIMORDIUM

An ecosystem with no designer. Everything you see is emergent.

WHAT IS THIS

Hundreds of organisms drift through a primordial soup. Each one carries a genome: body traits (size, speed, sense range, diet) and a tiny neural-network brain wired from random weights. Brains read the world — the direction and nearness of food, of other creatures, their own hunger — and decide how to turn and how hard to swim.

Nobody is taught anything. Creatures that happen to find food gain energy; with enough energy they split in two, and the child inherits the parent's genome with small random mutations. Those that starve vanish. That single loop — eat, reproduce, mutate, die — is natural selection, and it quietly tunes the whole population over a few minutes.

WHAT TO WATCH FOR

  • Foraging. Early on most creatures wander aimlessly. Watch the survivors start to steer toward food — a behaviour no one programmed.
  • Speciation. Colour is an inherited lineage marker. The species spectrum bar shows which lineages dominate; watch bands appear, split, and go extinct.
  • The trait chart. Real-time averages of size, speed, sense and carnivory. Selection pushes these lines — sometimes toward fast tiny grazers, sometimes toward big slow predators.
  • Predators. A high carnivory gene lets a creature eat smaller creatures whole. Watch food chains form and collapse.

PLAY WITH IT

  • Drag across the canvas to sow food and feed a region.
  • Shift-click to drop a brand-new random creature — an invasive species.
  • Turn food scarcity up and down to trigger booms and famines.
  • Crank mutation high for chaos, low to freeze evolution.
  • Toggle vision to see what a few creatures are sensing.
  • Push speed to 8× and let an hour of evolution pass in a minute.
